Weighted Dark Channel Dehazing

  • Mingzhu Zhu
  • Bingwei He
  • Jiantao Liu
A hazy day of Fuzhou University, China
and our haze removal result

Abstract

In dark channel based methods, local constant assumption is widely used to make the haze model and the prior invertible. It inevitably introduces defects since the mask or segmentation for the assumption can not perfectly avoid depth discontinuities and meanwhile cover enough pixels. Unfortunately, because of the limitation of the prior, which only confirms the existence of dark pixels but does not specify their locations or likelihood, no fidelity measurement is available in refinement thus the defects are either under-corrected or over-corrected in the final results. In this paper, we solve this delimma by introducing a straightforward weight map based on likely dark pixel and local constant likelihood. In the comparisons with state-of-the-art methods on two datasets and various samples, our method shows superiority on both simplicity and effectiveness. In the last, we discuss the failure cases and provide our method with an interface, which receives extra messages introduced by manual operations or other theories to tune the results.
